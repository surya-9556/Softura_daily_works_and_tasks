Content theory is a subset of motivational theories that try to define what motivates people.
Content theories of motivation often describe a system of needs that motivate peoples' actions.
While process theories of motivation attempt to explain how and why our motivations affect our behaviors,
content theories of motivation attempt to define what those motives or needs are.
